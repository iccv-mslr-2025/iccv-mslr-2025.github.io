## Summary

Sign language recognition has seen rapid advances in deep learning over the past five years, leveraging both vision-based and sensor-based approaches to achieve unprecedented accuracy and generalization. Recent work further pushes the envelope by integrating multimodal inputs—RGB video, depth, skeletal keypoints, facial expressions, and even radar data—to enhance robustness, privacy, and adaptability in real-world settings. Continuous sign language recognition remains especially challenging due to the need for temporal segmentation and data scarcity; novel weakly-supervised and cross-lingual techniques have begun to address these issues. At the same time, generative models and translation frameworks are emerging for sign language production, opening avenues for seamless communication between Deaf and hearing communities. Finally, foundational work in machine translation of sign languages highlights both the promise and the complexity of multi-channel articulation, underscoring the need for new benchmarks and metrics in this space.

## 📢 Call for Papers

We invite high-quality submissions full-papers on topics including, but not limited to:

### Core Recognition & Understanding
- Isolated & Continuous Sign Language Recognition: novel architectures for signer-independent operation, temporal segmentation, and feature extraction.
- Multimodal Fusion: strategies combining RGB, depth, skeletal keypoints, facial landmarks, and radar sensing for improved accuracy and privacy.
- Self-Supervised & Few-Shot Learning: low-shot, zero-shot, and domain adaptation methods for data-efficient sign recognition.

### Translation & Production
- Statistical & Neural MT for Sign Languages: end-to-end pipelines translating sign video to spoken/spoken-language text and vice versa.
- Generative Models for Sign Production: avatar-based and neural rendering approaches for fluent, context-aware sign synthesis.

### Datasets, Benchmarks & Metrics
- New Corpora & Evaluation Protocols: proposals for large-scale, diverse, and ethically sourced datasets; signer-independent benchmarks; metrics for robustness and fairness.
- Privacy-Preserving Sensing: radar-based or other non-visual modalities as alternatives to camera-based capture.

### Applications & Ethics
- Healthcare & Accessibility: sign language interfaces in medical settings, telemedicine, and assistive technologies.
- Ethical Considerations: bias mitigation, community co-design, and transparent data governance.

Submissions should follow the ICCV 2025 formatting guidelines and will undergo a double-blind review. Accepted papers will appear in the ICCV Workshop Proceedings.

## Call for Competition

The MSLR Challenge comprises two tracks, building on the MILAGE and WSLU initiatives:
- Track 1: Continuous SLR (RGB)
- Track 2: Isolated Multimodal SLR (RGB-D + Radar)

### Tasks
Signer-independent recognition and unseen-sentence generalization.

### Prizes & Publication
Top teams in each track will be recognized at the workshop with awards for Top-3 Winners per Track.
