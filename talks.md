# 🎤 Talks

## 🌴 Invited Speakers

### Prof. Richard Bowden, University of Surrey, UK  
[Scholar Profile](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=mvvgDvcAAAAJ)  
Prof. Richard Bowden is a distinguished scholar in the field of computer vision, with significant contributions to sign language recognition (SLR) and translation. As a Professor of Computer Vision and Machine Learning at the University of Surrey, he has led pioneering research on gesture and sign language recognition, human-computer interaction, and deep learning for visual understanding. His work has been instrumental in advancing automatic SLR systems, bridging the gap between artificial intelligence and accessibility for the deaf community. Prof. Bowden has published extensively in top-tier conferences and journals, with his research influencing both academic and industrial developments in SLR.

### Dr. Oscar Koller, Microsoft Inc., USA  
[Scholar Profile](https://scholar.google.com/citations?hl=it&user=vZrN9OgAAAAJ)  
Dr. Oscar Koller is a Principal Applied Scientist at Microsoft, specializing in the statistical modeling of signed and spoken languages. His work includes developing sign language and speech models that power several Microsoft and third-party products. Dr. Koller earned his PhD under the guidance of Prof. Ney (RWTH Aachen University), focusing on non-intrusive vision-based continuous sign language recognition. He co-created the RWTH-PHOENIX-Weather sign language corpus, a key resource in sign language research. Dr. Koller collaborates closely with the Deaf community and has basic knowledge of ASL and DGS. His commitment to Deaf accessibility began over 20 years ago with a volunteer stint at the Ashanti School for the Deaf in Ghana.

### Dr. Ehsan Hoque, Ministry of Defense, Saudi Arabia  
[Scholar Profile](https://scholar.google.com/citations?hl=it&user=ZJrR0KQAAAAJ)  
Ehsan Hoque (Ph.D. MIT, 2013) is a tenured Professor (on-leave) of Computer Science at the University of Rochester and co-leads the Rochester Human-Computer Interaction (ROC HCI) Lab. Hoque’s research focuses on AI-driven health technologies, human-centered computing, and leveraging AI to improve socioemotional skills and healthcare interactions. His honors include the MIT Technology Review Innovators Under 35 (TR35) award (2016), NSF CAREER Award (2018), Emerging Leader in Health and Medicine by the National Academy of Medicine (2020-2023), and ACM Ubiquitous and Pervasive Computing 10-Year Impact Award (2023). In 2025, he received the US Presidential Early Career Award for Scientists and Engineers.
